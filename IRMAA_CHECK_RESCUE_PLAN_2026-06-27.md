The site is not dead. It is getting discovered.

The problem is that Google is testing too many pages, mostly ranking them too low, and the few pages that are close to page 1 are not converting impressions into clicks. ## Main Diagnosis

1. The site is spreading authority too thin.

   There are many pages and tools, but the domain is still new. Google is testing lots of pages, yet most are ranking in positions 30 to 80. That means the site is visible but not trusted enough yet.

2. Broad keywords are too competitive right now.

   The site is not close for broad terms like:

   - `irmaa brackets 2026`
   - `medicare irmaa 2026`
   - `part b premium 2026`
   - `irmaa calculator`

   These are still worth targeting, but they should not be the only growth bet for a young site.

3. The best opportunity is the municipal bond page.

   `https://www.irmaacheck.com/municipal-bond-interest-irmaa/`

   This page has about 200 impressions and an average position around 8, but zero clicks. That is the clearest emergency. Google is already putting it near page 1, but the search result is not winning clicks.

4. A few small pages are already showing promise.

   These pages are the rescue base:

   - `/municipal-bond-interest-irmaa/`
   - `/do-both-spouses-pay-irmaa/`
   - `/how-long-does-irmaa-last/`
   - `/401k-withdrawal-medicare-premium-calculator/`
   - `/rmd-irmaa-calculator/`

   These should get the next round of work before creating more articles.

5. The homepage is matching a weird local query.

   Query: `irmaa planning nj`

   This produced 101 impressions but an average position around 70. That probably means Google sees the site as maybe relevant, but not locally relevant enough. We either need a real New Jersey page or we ignore this query and stop letting it distract us.

6. The site needs trust signals, not just content.

   Content alone is unlikely to rescue rankings quickly. IRMAA is a financial/Medicare topic, so Google needs stronger trust signals:

   - Clear sources
   - Better author/editor/about language
   - Original calculators and examples
   - More internal links
   - External backlinks from relevant sites
   - Better visual design and usability

- 10 relevant backlinks or resource mentions
- 25 organic clicks/week

### Phase 4 - Conversion Improvements

Timeframe: weeks 2 to 4, alongside authority work.

Goal: once visitors arrive, give them a reason to stay, use the calculator, and come back.

Actions:

1. Add stronger visual design to every article.

   Every article should have:

   - A relevant WebP hero image or visual
   - A simple chart, table, or scenario box
   - A clear calculator CTA above the fold
   - Related guides section

2. Add lead capture that matches intent.

   Examples:

   - Muni bond page: "Download the IRMAA MAGI checklist"
   - Roth page: "Get the Roth conversion IRMAA worksheet"
   - SSA-44 page: "Get the appeal timing checklist"
   - Brackets page: "Get the printable 2026 IRMAA chart"

3. Track events cleanly.

   Events to monitor:

   - calculator_start
   - calculator_result
   - checklist_download
   - newsletter_signup
   - sponsor_slot_click
   - outbound_source_click

4. Delay sponsor outreach.

   Sponsor monetization should wait until traffic and trust improve. The immediate monetization path should be lead capture, affiliate/resource partnerships, and building a credible audience.

## Stop Doing For Now

Stop creating new articles just because a keyword exists.

Stop trying to win all broad IRMAA keywords at once.

Stop prioritizing sponsor outreach before traffic exists.

Stop treating the homepage as the answer for every query.

Stop pushing visual polish to "later." For this niche, trust is visual too.
