# SEO Plan: IRMAA Surcharge Warning Calculator

## Strategy Summary

The site should launch as a calculator-first IRMAA resource for Medicare users who are worried that an income decision may raise their premiums. The fastest SEO path is not broad "Medicare" content. The opportunity is a focused cluster around IRMAA brackets, Medicare MAGI, income limits, appeals, and income-event warnings.

Primary positioning:

> Before a Roth conversion, RMD, home sale, stock sale, pension change, or other income event raises your Medicare premiums, check your IRMAA risk.

The first SEO cluster should balance three jobs:

- Capture high-volume bracket and definition traffic.
- Convert that traffic into calculator usage.
- Build trust with clear MAGI, appeal, and income-source explanations.

## Launch Page Plan

### 1. `/irmaa-brackets-2026`

Primary keyword:

- `irmaa brackets 2026`

Secondary keywords:

- `medicare irmaa brackets 2026`
- `part b irmaa 2026`
- `part d irmaa 2026`
- `irmaa thresholds 2026`
- `irmaa income limits 2026`
- `medicare surcharge 2026`
- `irmaa 2026`

Purpose:

This should be the highest-priority SEO landing page. It should explain the 2026 IRMAA brackets in plain English, show Part B and Part D surcharge tables, and push users into the calculator.

Page structure:

- H1: `2026 IRMAA Brackets: Medicare Part B and Part D Surcharges`
- Short answer: who pays IRMAA in 2026 and what income year is used.
- Prominent calculator module: "Check your estimated IRMAA bracket."
- Tables for single, married filing jointly, and married filing separately.
- "How much more could this cost per month and per year?"
- "Why your 2026 premium uses 2024 income."
- FAQs.
- Official source links to CMS, Medicare, and SSA.

Design notes:

- Put the calculator above or immediately below the first table.
- Use large, readable tables with sticky or repeated labels on mobile.
- Avoid dense footnotes near the top; use plain explanations first.

### 2. `/irmaa-calculator`

Primary keyword:

- `irmaa calculator`

Secondary keywords:

- `medicare surcharge calculator`
- `medicare premium income calculator`
- `medicare high income surcharge`

Purpose:

This is the core product page. It should answer: "Will my estimated income put me into an IRMAA bracket?"

Page structure:

- H1: `IRMAA Calculator: Estimate Your Medicare Premium Surcharge`
- Filing status.
- Estimated MAGI.
- Optional income events:
  - Roth conversion
  - RMD
  - IRA or 401(k) withdrawal
  - Capital gains
  - Home sale gain
  - Pension or part-time work
  - Tax-exempt interest
- Results:
  - Estimated IRMAA bracket.
  - Estimated Part B and Part D monthly surcharge.
  - Estimated annual surcharge.
  - Amount remaining before next bracket.
  - Printable summary.
- Disclaimer and official source links.

Design notes:

- Use a step-by-step form, not a spreadsheet-like interface.
- Make the result state visually calm: "Estimated bracket" and "Room before next bracket" should be easy to find.
- Use generous font sizes, high contrast, and clear focus states.

### 3. `/what-is-irmaa`

Primary keyword:

- `what is irmaa`

Secondary keywords:

- `medicare irmaa`
- `income related monthly adjustment amount`
- `medicare high income surcharge`

Purpose:

This is the beginner-friendly explanation page. It should define IRMAA without overwhelming users.

Page structure:

- H1: `What Is IRMAA? Medicare's Income-Related Premium Surcharge`
- Short definition.
- Who may pay it.
- Why it affects both Part B and Part D.
- Why income from two years ago is used.
- Link to 2026 brackets.
- Link to calculator.
- FAQs:
  - Is IRMAA a tax?
  - Is IRMAA monthly or annual?
  - Does IRMAA apply to Medicare Advantage?
  - Can IRMAA change each year?

### 4. `/medicare-magi`

Primary keyword:

- `medicare magi`

Secondary keywords:

- `irmaa magi`
- `what is magi for medicare`
- `what income is used for medicare premiums`
- `is irmaa based on agi or taxable income`
- `what counts as income for irmaa`

Purpose:

This page explains the income number used for IRMAA. It should prevent a common user mistake: assuming IRMAA is based on taxable income after deductions.

Page structure:

- H1: `Medicare MAGI: What Income Counts for IRMAA?`
- Short answer: IRMAA generally uses AGI plus tax-exempt interest.
- Explain where these numbers appear on a tax return.
- Income sources that usually count:
  - Taxable Social Security benefits
  - Wages
  - Pension income
  - Traditional IRA and 401(k) withdrawals
  - RMDs
  - Roth conversions
  - Capital gains
  - Interest and dividends
  - Tax-exempt interest
- Income sources that may not count or need careful handling:
  - Roth IRA qualified withdrawals
  - HSA distributions for qualified medical expenses
  - QCDs made correctly from an IRA
- Calculator CTA.

### 5. `/how-to-avoid-irmaa`

Primary keyword:

- `how to avoid irmaa`

Secondary keywords:

- `how to reduce irmaa`
- `does qcd reduce irmaa`
- `qualified charitable distribution irmaa`

Purpose:

This page should be practical but conservative. It should not give personalized financial advice. It should explain common planning ideas and encourage users to check with a tax or financial professional.

Page structure:

- H1: `How to Avoid IRMAA Surprises Before Medicare Premiums Rise`
- Explain that users often cannot "avoid" IRMAA after income has already happened, but they can plan future income.
- Strategies:
  - Estimate MAGI before large income events.
  - Watch Roth conversions.
  - Coordinate RMDs and withdrawals.
  - Manage capital gains timing.
  - Consider QCDs if eligible.
  - Understand tax-exempt interest.
  - Review whether an appeal may apply after certain life-changing events.
- Calculator CTA after each major strategy section.

### 6. `/irmaa-appeal-ssa-44-form`

Primary keyword:

- `ssa 44 form`

Secondary keywords:

- `irmaa appeal`
- `irmaa life changing event`

Purpose:

This page captures appeal-related traffic and helps users understand when SSA Form SSA-44 may apply.

Page structure:

- H1: `SSA-44 Form: When You Can Appeal an IRMAA Medicare Surcharge`
- Explain what SSA-44 is.
- Explain common life-changing events.
- Explain that not every one-time income event qualifies.
- Link to official SSA form and instructions.
- Link to calculator for future planning.

Compliance note:

Be especially careful on this page. Do not imply that a Roth conversion, home sale, or capital gain automatically qualifies for appeal relief.

## Second-Wave Calculator Pages

These pages may show little or no measurable Ubersuggest volume, but they map directly to high-intent user situations. They should reuse the main calculator engine with preselected context and scenario-specific copy.

### `/roth-conversion-irmaa-calculator`

Target questions:

- Will a Roth conversion trigger IRMAA?
- How much can I convert before IRMAA?
- Does a Roth conversion affect Medicare premiums?

Angle:

Roth conversions are often planned intentionally, so users can estimate before acting.

### `/rmd-irmaa-calculator`

Target questions:

- Do RMDs affect Medicare premiums?
- Can an RMD trigger IRMAA?
- How do RMDs affect Medicare MAGI?

Angle:

RMDs can be predictable, making them good for annual planning.

### `/capital-gains-irmaa-calculator`

Target questions:

- Do capital gains affect Medicare premiums?
- Will selling stock trigger IRMAA?
- Can investment gains raise Medicare Part B premiums?

Angle:

Capital gains can push a retiree over a bracket even when regular income feels modest.

### `/home-sale-medicare-premium-calculator`

Target questions:

- Will selling my home affect Medicare premiums?
- Does home sale gain count for IRMAA?
- Can a house sale raise Medicare premiums?

Angle:

This is an anxiety-heavy page. Explain that the taxable gain, not the sale price, is the key number.

### `/ira-withdrawal-medicare-premium-calculator`

Target questions:

- Does an IRA withdrawal affect Medicare premiums?
- Can a retirement withdrawal trigger IRMAA?

### `/401k-withdrawal-medicare-premium-calculator`

Target questions:

- Do 401(k) withdrawals affect Medicare premiums?
- Can a 401(k) withdrawal increase Medicare Part B premiums?

### `/qcd-irmaa`

Target questions:

- Does a QCD reduce IRMAA?
- Can a qualified charitable distribution lower Medicare premiums?

Angle:

Useful as a supporting article from the "how to avoid IRMAA" page.

## Internal Linking Plan

Every article should link to:

- `/irmaa-calculator`
- `/irmaa-brackets-2026`
- `/medicare-magi`

The calculator should link back to:

- Current-year bracket page.
- Medicare MAGI explainer.
- Scenario-specific calculators.
- Appeal page, but only after explaining that appeals have limited eligibility.

Suggested hub flow:

1. `/what-is-irmaa` introduces the concept.
2. `/medicare-magi` explains the income calculation.
3. `/irmaa-brackets-2026` shows current thresholds.
4. `/irmaa-calculator` lets users estimate their situation.
5. Event calculators handle specific income decisions.
6. `/how-to-avoid-irmaa` and `/irmaa-appeal-ssa-44-form` handle planning and after-the-fact concerns.

## Content Rules

- Put the practical answer before the technical explanation.
- Use "estimated" and "educational" consistently.
- Avoid fear-based copy.
- Avoid saying the tool can prevent surcharges. Use "estimate," "check," "plan," and "understand."
- Use official source links where thresholds, forms, and definitions matter.
- Update current-year threshold pages annually.
- Date-stamp pages that depend on a tax or Medicare year.

## Senior-Friendly Design Direction

The design should feel calm, legible, and trustworthy rather than flashy.

Recommended style:

- Large base text, ideally 18px or higher.
- High contrast text and controls.
- Clear step labels.
- Plain language field names.
- Large tap targets.
- Minimal animation.
- No dense dashboard layout.
- No tiny tooltip-only explanations.
- Printable summaries with clean black-and-white output.

Calculator UX:

- Ask for one idea at a time.
- Explain "MAGI" beside the input, but do not bury the user in tax language.
- Let users add income events with labeled rows.
- Show monthly and annual impact together.
- Show "room before next bracket" as a plain-English warning.

Visual tone:

- Trustworthy civic-finance feel.
- Soft but not decorative.
- Avoid dark-mode-first styling.
- Avoid aggressive red warning states unless the user is over a threshold.

## Unified Template Strategy

The site uses one brand system with different page templates by search intent:

- Calculator and homepage pages use the Guided Financial Planner template.
- Bracket and official-reference pages use the Calm Civic Utility template.
- Blog and SEO explainer pages use the Editorial Medicare Guide template.

This is not an A/B split and not three standalone site versions. It is one information architecture with specialized page layouts.

## Ad Placement Strategy

Ad inventory should be present from the start but restrained, labeled, and subordinate to trust.

- Calculator and homepage: top sponsor strip, education-partner block below the result area, and optional bottom banner after the bracket preview. Ads must not push the calculator below the fold.
- Bracket and official-reference pages: quiet leaderboard near the top and right-rail ad beside source/table content. These pages should remain table-forward and official-feeling.
- Blog and SEO explainer pages: right-rail ad stack, related guides, and one in-content ad separator after the main answer. Ads should not interrupt the first answer or compliance disclaimer.

## Scenario Page Positioning

The scenario calculator pages are not primary SEO bets for launch. Ubersuggest shows little or no measurable search volume for most event-specific phrases. Treat these pages as UX entry flows from the main calculator, bracket page, and planning articles. Their job is to help motivated users model a specific income event, not to carry the first wave of organic acquisition.
